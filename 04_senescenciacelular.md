## The hidden truth (with sources)

- **Estrogen suppresses cellular senescence.** It protects against DNA damage, reduces oxidative stress, suppresses ROS via mitochondrial estrogen receptors, and prevents the SASP (senescence-associated secretory phenotype). [Source: ScienceDirect 2022, Evidence of sex differences in cellular senescence]
- Following menopause, women show significant **increases in telomere shortening** and reduced anti-inflammatory macrophage activity. [Source: Walker et al. 2016; Toniolo et al. 2015]
- Menopausal hormone therapy (KEEPS trial, Mayo Clinic) **lowered serum levels of senescence markers** GDF15, TNFR1, and FAS in women on both oral conjugated equine estrogen and transdermal 17β-estradiol vs. placebo. [Source: Mayo Clinic / PMC KEEPS Study 2020]
- Postmenopausal women have **2 to 6 times higher CVD incidence** than premenopausal women of the same age (Framingham cohort). 17β-estradiol delays arterial senescence via Rab9-dependent mitochondrial autophagy. [Source: AHA Journal 2021]
- Bilateral oophorectomy before age 45 **increases risk of multi-morbidity** and accelerated aging — strong evidence estrogen is a longevity hormone. [Source: KEEPS Study]
- Senolytics research (dasatinib + quercetin, fisetin) is showing potential for ovarian aging and reproductive longevity. [Source: npj Aging 2024]
- Estrogen prevents bone cell senescence through Usp10-dependent p53 degradation — explaining why osteoporosis is a female disease. [Source: Springer Cell and Tissue Research 2021]
- The skin is the most visible senescence organ in women; estrogen loss accelerates collagen degradation, dermal thinning, and the SASP in skin fibroblasts.
